Proudly Serving Since 1921

Original ARRL Charter of Affiliation – Signed February 17, 1922

Our original charter, signed by Hiram Percy Maxim himself — one of the oldest continuously active clubs in the ARRL.

Founded March 10, 1921

Born in the spark-gap era when Reading had ~35 wireless experimenters and a brand-new breed of licensed radio amateurs. Informal meetings began at the old Reading Company YMCA at 8th & Green Streets.

ARRL Affiliated February 17, 1922

One of the earliest clubs to join the American Radio Relay League. Re-affiliated in 1941 and proudly remains an ARRL Special Service Club to this day.

Our Callsign Legacy

  • 1949–1964 → W3PFT
  • 1964–1965 → W3CCH (memorial to Stanton L. Bast)
  • 1965–present → W3BN (memorial to James Marx, club president & benefactor)

Who We Are Today

Over 100 years later, we’re still the same club — just with better gear. We provide emergency communications, public service for events, weekly nets, POTA activations, fox hunts, license testing, and technical mentoring.

We’re called “amateurs” because we’re not paid — we do it for the love of radio. But when the lights go out, our communications are professional.

Licensing & Education

Our FCC-certified VE team has administered 85 exam sessions and helped 763 hams earn or upgrade their licenses over the past 30+ years. Many went on to careers in electronics, broadcasting, and public safety — all because someone at the Reading Radio Club took the time to teach them.

Always Ready

Weekly equipment tests. A 146.910 repeater with 30-mile coverage. Members who can be on the air in minutes when disaster strikes. That’s been our promise since 1921 — and it still is.

Meetings are open to everyone — licensed or not, young or old.
